# Cataloging my collection of CDs and vinyl records

Music is more than just sound—it's a story, a mood, and a reflection of who we are. With that in mind, the idea behind my [music collection](https://www.080f53.com/music-collection) project was simple: Combine my passion for music with my love for documenting everything, including the unordinary.

<!-- truncate -->

By using Docusaurus, I can showcase details about my collection of CDs and vinyl records in an easy-to-browse way. Visitors can easily browse or search my music collection and see specific details about which CDs and vinyl records that I own.

## Why I chose to catalog my collection of CDs and vinyl records

Unlike with digital music-streaming services, when you buy physical copies of music, you own a copy of that music. However, keeping track of what you own can be difficult, especially if there are multiple versions of a product available.

I chose to catalog my music collection so that I could:

- **Catalog my music collection:** This project is a digital keepsake for my growing collection of CDs and vinyl records, and is available wherever I go.
- **Share what albums I own:** I can share not only my collection but also my tastes in music with others.
- **Document the unordinary:** Cataloging album information is significantly different from writing tutorials and guides. Because of this, showcasing this type of information requires a different type of creativity.

## What kinds of details I chose to include about each album

In my [Music collection](https://www.080f53.com/music-collection/) project, you'll find an evolving catalog of CDs and vinyl records, each of which include the following details:

| Details                | Options                                                                                                    |
|:-----------------------|:-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Format**             | CD or Vinyl                                                                                                |
| **Country**            | Country of origin                                                                                          |
| **Released**           | Year the album was released                                                                                |
| **Case**               | **CDs:** Digipak, Eco pack, Gatefold paper sleeve, Jewel case, or Mini-LP sleeve<br />**Vinyl:** Cardboard |
| **Obi**                | Yes or No                                                                                                  |
| **Additional details** | Link to a reputable site, like [Discogs](https://www.discogs.com)                                          |  

:::info

An obi is a strip of paper around the spine of a CD case that's used to advertise the CD. When reselling used CDs, collectors often favor CDs with an obi, especially if the CD is uncommon or rare and/or if the obi is from a specific series.

:::

## How I update my music collection

This project requires a few steps, some of which are automated. The process is as follows:

1. In the [music-collection](https://github.com/josh-wong/music-collection) repository, I:
  1. Create a branch.
  1. Add, update, or remove albums or album information
  1. Create a PR that targets the **main** branch.
  1. Confirm the changes in the PR.
  1. Merge the PR.
1. The [syncing workflow](https://github.com/josh-wong/music-collection/blob/main/.github/workflows/sync-docs.yml) automatically syncs the updates as a branch to [josh-wong.github.io](https://github.com/josh-wong/josh-wong.github.io), my website repository.
1. The [auto-PR workflow for my music-collection](https://github.com/josh-wong/josh-wong.github.io/blob/main/.github/workflows/auto-create-pr-cd-component.yml) automatically creates a PR.
1. If the checks in that PR run successfully, I merge the PR.
1. GitHub Pages builds and deploys my site by using a [workflow for Docusaurus](https://github.com/josh-wong/josh-wong.github.io/blob/main/.github/workflows/deploy.yml).

In addition, the [music-collection](https://github.com/josh-wong/music-collection) syncs to my personal notes in Obsidian. By having the same Markdown files available in Obsidian as well, I can browse my music collection in my private, note-taking environment and link to albums from my other notes.
